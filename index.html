How it works

Pieces ride in on the belt. You stack them into a trailer wall that has to hold up — support every box, keep the load balanced, lock complete rows into shelves, load hazmat low, and seal the wall before dispatch.

Build the wall

Select freight, place it, rotate it. Floor-up, support underneath, no floating boxes — the physics won't let you cheat gravity.

Load it right

Lock shelves, keep the center of gravity centered, fragile on top, hazmat below the waist line, and stuff the dead air inside tires.

Get your grade

Every wall is scored on integrity, density, pace, and safety — and ranked, from Rookie to Yard Legend.

What you're actually learning

Under the game is real freight-handling intuition — the stuff that separates a loader who packs tight and safe from one who buries the fragile and drops the hazmat.

Support & balance

Nothing floats. Keep the center of gravity centered so the load doesn't shift the second the door cracks.

Lock your shelves

Fill a full row and it locks — a braced shelf that carries the weight above it. Stagger the seams like brickwork.

Hazmat & fragile

Dangerous goods ride low and capped (49 CFR intuition); fragile rides on top. Bury either and it's a write-up.

Density is the job

Pack tight, fill the dead air, use smalls and bags for the gaps big freight can't. Empty space is wasted freight.

Pace under pressure

A dispatch clock and a load-rate that managers actually track. Fast and clean beats fast and sloppy.

Read what you're handed

Freight doesn't flow clean. Rotate, re-stage, and find the lock in an awkward piece instead of forcing it.

FAQ

Is LOCK IN free?

Yes. The full game is free forever, no login required. Workers and students never pay. The program dashboard — for schools and employers who want the metrics — is live, and the first pilot programs get free access while we onboard.

What does it teach?

Load theory — support and balance, locking complete rows into shelves, keeping the center of gravity centered, fragile on top, hazmat below the waist line, and using the dead air inside tires. Every wall is scored on integrity, density, pace, and safety.

What's the daily challenge?

One wall, the same freight and clock for everyone on Earth, resetting at 00:00 UTC. Everybody plays the identical puzzle and posts to one shared world board. It's a two-minute daily brain-warmup — many classrooms use it to get into work mode.

Do I need experience to start?

No. Training walks you through a full wall one placement at a time — you watch a trainer build it the right way and sign off when you're ready, exactly like a real first day on the dock. Then you load your own.

Who is the dashboard for?

CTE programs (Perkins-eligible), trade and technical schools, and warehouse operations teams. It reports opted-in students' loading competencies: attention to detail, follow-through, throughput, and safety. It's live at trailer-load.com/dashboard — if that's your program, ask about a pilot.

Does it replace safety training?

No. LOCK IN is a training supplement that builds intuition and tracks readiness. It does not replace employer safety training, OSHA training, or any certification requirement.
